Automating the SPPB in clinical workflows

Manual SPPB administration is effective but can become inconsistent at scale. This case study summarizes where variability appears in routine use and how automated capture supports standardized, repeatable measurement across operators and sites.

Where inconsistency happens in manual SPPB

When tests are administered by different staff members across days or sites, small protocol differences can accumulate and affect comparability.

Timing

Stopwatch variability

Start/stop timing decisions differ across operators, especially in gait speed and repeated chair stands.

Instruction

Protocol drift

Verbal cues, demonstration style, and setup details can drift, changing participant behavior and performance.

Scale

Training burden

Sustaining consistent administration requires ongoing training and quality checks as teams change.

How automation helps

Automation is not about replacing clinical judgment. It reduces avoidable measurement noise by standardizing steps that are typically rater-dependent.

Standardization

Guided, repeatable flow

A fixed sequence with consistent instructions and timing logic supports comparable results across visits and sites.

  • Consistent setup and prompts.
  • Automated capture of timing and sensor signals.
  • Structured export for reporting and analysis.
Scalability

Designed for throughput

Practical administration by non-specialist staff supports broader deployment in clinics and community programs.
