Use case: occupational health and work ability programs

Many employers and occupational health services run periodic health checks and targeted programs for mobility, strength, and injury-risk reduction. A standardized functional test battery can help teams communicate status, set measurable goals, and monitor improvement over time.

Screening

Identify functional limitations

A quick battery helps flag low mobility or reduced lower-limb power that may affect work demands.

Programs

Set measurable targets

Component-level results help define practical goals for strength, balance, and mobility interventions.

Return-to-work

Track recovery consistently

Repeat the same protocol to document functional change during rehabilitation and reconditioning.

Implementation notes

Standard operating procedure

  • Use one protocol across sites to reduce operator-driven variation.
  • Define repeat-testing intervals aligned with program duration.
  • Document mobility aids and footwear rules for comparability.

Data handling and privacy

  • Collect only what is needed for the program’s purpose and consent scope.
  • Use de-identified participant IDs for program reporting when possible.
  • Separate clinical notes from aggregate employer reporting.
Outcome

Clear, shareable summaries

A simple “baseline vs follow-up” view helps program managers communicate progress.

Operations

Lower training burden

Consistent test flow supports reliable delivery by occupational health staff.

Scale

Repeatable across cohorts

Works for periodic check-ups, targeted groups, or evaluation studies.

Validation context

AndanteFit is an example of an automated system designed to operationalize the SPPB in clinical and research settings. Its validation involved Dr. Jack Guralnik, who originally developed the SPPB during his work at the U.S. National Institutes of Health. In addition, Professor Alfonso Cruz-Jentoft and colleagues from the Global Leadership Initiative on Sarcopenia (GLIS) participated in the validation process, supporting its applicability in studies involving older adults.
