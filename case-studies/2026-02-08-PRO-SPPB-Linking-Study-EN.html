Research Brief

Comparing Self-Reported Physical Function (PRO) and SPPB Performance
on a Shared Common Scale (T-score)

February 08, 2026 · DYPHI Research Team
Standardized Linking of Self-Report

Researchers at Charité University Medicine Berlin demonstrated that SPPB results can be interpreted on the same axis as the standard PRO measure, PROMIS Physical Function T-score.

In clinical and research settings, patient-reported outcomes (PRO) and performance-based assessments (SPPB) are often used in parallel, but their differing scales make direct comparison and integrated interpretation challenging.

These findings reframe the two assessments not as interchangeable alternatives, but as linkable measures, providing a foundation for summarizing and reporting both in a common language.

Key Message
Different measurement approaches (self-report, performance assessment) can be compared and communicated on the same standard reference.
Clinical Point
An SPPB score of 9 can be reported in standardized language as "below-average function (T-score ~40)."
Clinical Application
AndanteFit supports consistent, protocol-based data collection in eSPPB format.

SPPB 9 ≈ T-score 40.0
Describing below-average function level in standardized language

The study shows that SPPB scores can be interpreted on the same axis as PROMIS Physical Function T-scores. In particular, an SPPB score of 9 corresponds to approximately T-score 40.0, which can be described as 1 standard deviation below the mean (50).

Interpretation note: SPPB and PRO can be organized into a form that allows comparison on a single common reference.
