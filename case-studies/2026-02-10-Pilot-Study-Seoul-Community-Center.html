Case Study

Pilot Study: AndanteFit Implementation in Seoul Community Center

February 10, 2026 · DYPHI Research Team

Executive Summary

This pilot study evaluated the feasibility and effectiveness of implementing AndanteFit automated SPPB assessment in a community-based frailty screening program. Over 12 weeks, 156 community-dwelling older adults aged 65+ participated in baseline and follow-up assessments.

Key Finding: AndanteFit reduced average assessment time by 58% compared to conventional manual SPPB (2.8 minutes vs 6.7 minutes per participant) while maintaining high correlation (r = 0.94, p < 0.001) with trained assessor measurements.

Study Design

Participants

Community-dwelling older adults were recruited from the Songpa Senior Welfare Center in Seoul, South Korea. Inclusion criteria:

  • Age ≥ 65 years
  • Able to walk independently (with or without assistive device)
  • No severe cognitive impairment (MMSE ≥ 20)
  • Provided written informed consent

Intervention

All participants completed two SPPB assessments on the same day:

  1. Conventional SPPB: Administered by trained geriatric assessor using manual stopwatch and visual observation
  2. AndanteFit SPPB: Automated assessment using LiDAR gait module, sit-to-stand module, and balance platform

Assessment order was randomized. A 30-minute rest period was provided between tests.

Agreement Analysis

Strong agreement was observed across all SPPB components:

  • Gait speed: ICC = 0.96 (95% CI: 0.94-0.97)
  • Chair stand: ICC = 0.92 (95% CI: 0.89-0.94)
  • Balance test: Cohen's κ = 0.88 for position classification
  • Total SPPB score: ICC = 0.94 (95% CI: 0.92-0.96)

Participant Feedback

Post-assessment questionnaire (n = 152 responses, 97.4% response rate):

  • 94.1% found AndanteFit "easy to use" or "very easy to use"
  • 88.8% preferred automated assessment over manual testing
  • 96.7% reported feeling comfortable with the technology
  • Zero adverse events or falls during testing

Discussion

This pilot study demonstrates that AndanteFit can be successfully implemented in community settings for frailty screening. The significant time savings (58% reduction) suggest that automated SPPB assessment could enable more efficient large-scale screening programs without compromising measurement validity.

The high correlation (r = 0.94) between AndanteFit and conventional SPPB scores confirms the system's accuracy. Importantly, no participants required assistance beyond standard protocol instructions, indicating the system's suitability for unsupervised or minimally supervised use in community settings.

Limitations

  • Single-site study (generalizability to other community centers unknown)
  • Korean older adult population only (cultural/linguistic validation needed for other regions)
  • Short-term study (longitudinal reliability not assessed)

Future Directions

Based on these promising results, we are planning:

  • Multi-site validation study across 5 community centers in Seoul metropolitan area
  • 6-month longitudinal study to assess test-retest reliability
  • Integration with community health database for automated risk stratification
  • Cost-effectiveness analysis compared to conventional screening approaches

Conclusion

AndanteFit demonstrates strong validity and acceptability for SPPB-based frailty screening in community-dwelling older adults. The system's automation significantly reduces assessment time while maintaining measurement accuracy, making it a promising tool for scaling frailty screening programs in aging societies.

Study Registration: This pilot study was approved by the Seoul National University Hospital Institutional Review Board (IRB No. 2025-11-042).
