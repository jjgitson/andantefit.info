AceBiome Researchers Publish BNR17 Pilot Study in Older Adults: Potential for Physical Function and Body Composition Improvements

SPPB Total Score Significantly Improved After 8 Weeks; Increases in Lean Mass and Lower-Limb Muscle Mass, Reductions in Body Weight, Body Fat, and Visceral Fat Confirmed

A pilot study recently published by AceBiome researchers suggests that supplementation with Lactobacillus gasseri BNR17 may produce positive changes in physical function and body composition in older adults. The study enrolled 46 community-dwelling older adults with a mean age of 69.1 years, who consumed BNR17 once daily for 8 weeks. Researchers compared Short Physical Performance Battery (SPPB) scores, grip strength, and body composition measures before and after the supplementation period.

SPPB total score, assessed using the AndanteFit system, increased significantly from 10.74 at baseline to 11.37 at 8 weeks. The authors suggested that BNR17 supplementation may support overall physical function improvement in older adults. They also interpreted the observed 0.63-point SPPB increase as falling within the range of small but clinically meaningful change described in the existing literature.

Positive changes were also observed in muscle mass-related measures. Both total lean mass and soft lean mass increased significantly. Segment-specific analysis revealed a significant increase in lower-limb muscle mass. In contrast, no significant differences were found in upper-limb or trunk muscle mass.

Body fat and obesity-related parameters also improved. Body weight decreased from 65.28 kg to 64.63 kg, and body fat mass declined from 22.62 kg to 22.02 kg. BMI decreased from 25.08 to 24.86, and the obesity rate decreased from 116.67% to 115.74%, both significantly. Among abdominal obesity indicators, visceral fat area decreased significantly from 100.44 cm² to 97.39 cm².

The authors stated that these findings demonstrate the multifaceted potential of BNR17 in terms of muscle function and metabolic health. Physical function was assessed via SPPB and body composition via BIA-based analysis, with positive changes observed across both functional and compositional dimensions.

However, the researchers also noted that this was a single-arm before-after pilot study. The design lacked a control group, the study period was relatively short at 8 weeks, and the sample size of 46 was not large, warranting caution in interpreting the results. They also noted that dietary and lifestyle factors were not separately controlled, and that body composition assessed via BIA may be influenced by hydration status and other measurement conditions. The research team indicated that the effects would need to be further validated through larger-scale randomized controlled trials.

This study is meaningful as early clinical evidence suggesting that BNR17 may contribute to physical function improvement and body composition changes in older adults. SPPB assessment in this study was performed using DYPHI's AndanteFit system. AndanteFit is currently used in three clinical trials related to sarcopenia treatments and in human application trials of functional foods targeting muscle and strength, expanding its role as a tool for more objective and standardized assessment of physical function.
