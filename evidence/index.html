Evidence and publications

Selected peer-reviewed studies related to automated physical performance assessment.

Key Peer-Reviewed Studies

Validation of a Multi-Sensor-Based Kiosk for Short Physical Performance Battery Guralnik included
Journal of the American Geriatrics Society, 2019 (67(12), 2605–2609).
Hee-Won Jung, Hyunchul Roh, Younggun Cho, Jinyong Jeong, Young-Sik Shin, Jae-Young Lim, Jack M. Guralnik, Jihong Park.

Objective summary: This study validated a multi-sensor-based kiosk designed to automate standardized administration of SPPB components in older adults, and demonstrated strong agreement with conventional assessments conducted by trained personnel.

DOI: 10.1111/jgs.16135
Functional Age Predicted by Electronic Short Physical Performance Battery Can Detect Frailty Status in Older Adults Guralnik included
Clinical Interventions in Aging, 2020 (15, 2175–2182).
Hee-Won Jung, Taeyang Jin, Ji Yeon Baek, Seongjun Yoon, Eunju Lee, Jack M. Guralnik, Il-Young Jang.

Objective summary: This study proposed a functional age metric derived from electronically captured SPPB data and demonstrated its ability to discriminate frailty status in community-dwelling older adults.

DOI: 10.2147/CIA.S280542
Comparison of Two Electronic Physical Performance Batteries by Measurement Time and Sarcopenia Classification
Sensors, 2021 (21(15), 5147).
Chan Mi Park, Hee-Won Jung, Il-Young Jang, Ji Yeon Baek, Seongjun Yoon, Hyunchul Roh, Eunju Lee.

Objective summary: This study compared two electronic physical performance assessment systems in terms of testing time efficiency and sarcopenia classification, providing evidence on the feasibility of electronic SPPB-based assessments.

DOI: 10.3390/s21155147
Comparison of Human Interpretation and a Rule-Based Algorithm for Instrumented Sit-to-Stand Test
Annals of Geriatric Medicine and Research, 2021 (25(2), 86–92).
Hee-Won Jung, Seongjun Yoon, Ji Yeon Baek, Eunju Lee, Il-Young Jang, Hyunchul Roh.

Objective summary: This study evaluated agreement between human raters and a rule-based algorithm applied to instrumented sit-to-stand data, supporting algorithm-based interpretation of lower-extremity performance.

DOI: 10.4235/agmr.21.0034
Development and Validation of 2D-LiDAR-Based Gait Analysis Instrument and Algorithm
Sensors, 2021 (21, 414).
Seongjun Yoon, Hee-Won Jung, Heeyoune Jung, Keewon Kim, Suk-Koo Hong, Hyunchul Roh, Byung-Mo Oh.

Objective summary: This study developed and validated a 2D-LiDAR-based system for automated gait analysis, providing technical foundations for sensor-based gait speed measurement relevant to SPPB.

DOI: 10.3390/s21020414
Validation of a Multi-Sensor-Based Kiosk in the Use of the Short Physical Performance Battery in Older Adults Attending a Fall and Balance Clinic
Annals of Geriatric Medicine and Research, 2022 (26(2), 125–133).
Herb Howard C. Hernandez, Eng Hui Ong, Louise Heyzer, Cai Ning Tan, Faezah Ghazali, Daphne Zihui Yang, Hee-Won Jung, Noor Hafizah Ismail, Wee Shiong Lim.

Objective summary: This study validated the clinical usability of a multi-sensor-based SPPB kiosk in a fall and balance clinic setting, supporting its applicability in real-world geriatric assessment workflows.

DOI: 10.4235/agmr.22.0022
