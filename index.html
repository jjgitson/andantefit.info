Automated SPPB platform

Standardize physical performance assessment at scale

AndanteFit operationalizes the Short Physical Performance Battery (SPPB) for clinical workflows and research protocols, with automated capture of gait speed, five-times sit-to-stand, and balance.

Designed for repeatable measurement

When studies scale to multiple sites and multiple assessors, measurement consistency becomes a primary risk. AndanteFit is built to reduce variation by standardizing timing, instructions, and sensor capture.

A practical protocol, from check-in to reporting

Validation anchored in the SPPB community

AndanteFit is an example of an automated system designed to operationalize the SPPB in clinical and research settings. Its validation involved Dr. Jack Guralnik, who originally developed the SPPB during his work at the U.S. National Institutes of Health. In addition, Professor Alfonso Cruz-Jentoft and colleagues from the Global Leadership Initiative on Sarcopenia (GLIS) participated in the validation process, supporting its applicability in studies involving older adults.
