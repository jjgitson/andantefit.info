Assessment Components

Short Physical Performance Battery (SPPB)

The SPPB is a standardized, validated assessment tool for evaluating lower extremity function in older adults. It consists of three objective performance tests.

🚶

Gait Speed Test

4-meter walking test measuring functional mobility. AndanteFit supports both static and dynamic start conditions with continuous LiDAR-based velocity tracking.

🪑

Chair Stand Test

Five repetitions of sit-to-stand movements assessing lower limb strength. Automated detection eliminates observer timing variability.

⚖️

Balance Test

Progressive standing balance positions (side-by-side, semi-tandem, tandem). Precise posture monitoring ensures protocol compliance.

Clinical Applications

Used across clinical settings and community programs

SPPB-based physical performance assessment is used in a wide range of conditions and settings related to older adult health, supported by international guidelines and research.

Sarcopenia diagnosis

Used in sarcopenia guidelines across Europe, the U.S., and Asia, and applied as a key outcome measure in intervention studies.

Frailty screening

Included as a physical performance assessment tool in the WHO ICOPE guidelines for community screening.

Cancer rehabilitation

Recommended as a key physical performance measure in comprehensive geriatric assessment (CGA) for older cancer patients (AOS, SIOG, etc.).

Cardiac rehabilitation

Included in clinical guidelines to assess lower-limb function and mobility during cardiac rehabilitation (Japan, Europe, etc.).

Clinical trials

Used for participant screening and outcome evaluation in intervention studies on strength and functional improvement.

Population-based studies

Used in large-scale health surveys and cohort studies in the U.S., Korea, Europe, and other regions.

Pain treatment monitoring

Used to objectively track functional recovery after spine-related pain interventions.

Chronic disease management

Applied to monitor functional changes in patients with heart failure, chronic kidney disease, COPD, diabetes, and more.

Neurological rehabilitation

Used to assess mobility and lower-limb function in patients with stroke, Parkinson’s disease, dementia, and related conditions.

Fall risk management

Used in rehabilitation and long-term care settings for fall risk screening and functional maintenance programs.

Pre- and postoperative assessment

Used to evaluate surgical risk before procedures and to monitor recovery after surgery in older patients.

Older driver assessment

Used to evaluate mobility and lower-limb function for driving fitness and safety programs in older adults.
